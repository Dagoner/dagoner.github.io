Task requirement

How to engage with the CO2 footprints from office meetings? In this task we will look at how Cognite can reduce its own emissions connected to Cognite employees’ behaviour and activities, and focus on the climate gas emissions related to how we run our meetings. By communicating with Cognite’s employees we would like to:

  • Reduce the amount of CO2 emitted due to internal and external meetings
  • Create awareness among Cognite employees on climate gas emissions
  • And hopefully, make an “awareness transition” to our work with customers so that environmental topics more easily are included in the work with them.

Background research

How to arrange the meeting

By talking with Cognite, we know that the colleagues of Cognite use Slack and google calender to book and inform the meetings.

Type of meeting

  • digital meeting
  • physical meeting

CO2 emissions of meeting

What are the emission factors to be used when calculating CO2 emissions related to meetings:

  • Heating/cooling/electricity of meeting rooms=heating/cooling/electricity of distributed work=0,003 kg CO2/m2 per hour (=standard rate)
  • Internet use for video streaming= 0,015 kg CO2/hour (NO energy mix）
  • Commuting to work (for physical meetings)= 0,027 kg CO2/km travelled per passenger (bus)
  • Business travel for physical meeting at external premises= 0,270 kg CO2/km travelled per passenger (by airplane, average distance (to US))

Insights

If it’s in working time, no matter if there is digital meeting, CO2 emissions should be the same, because the colleagues will still use the computers and other devices anyway no matter if they have the meeting. So we only need to focus on the emissions of physical meeting whcih include:

  • CO2 of using the house
  • CO2 of travel
